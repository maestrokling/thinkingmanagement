About

Twenty years in enterprise technology taught me one thing that keeps proving itself: the organizations that win aren't the ones with the best tools. They're the ones that build the operational and cultural infrastructure to actually use them.

At AWS, I built the Southeast professional services practice from 5 to 60+ people, launched the Healthcare vertical, and turned a decade of losses into a profitable practice with 100% customer retention. At Microsoft, I led cloud transformation across 60+ enterprise accounts and turned 6 critical escalations into platform expansions. I founded Allies@ at Amazon because culture isn't a side project — it's load-bearing infrastructure.

The pattern was always the same: the technology was never the hard part. The hard part was alignment, capability, and the change management that separates a pilot from a practice.

I've been writing about the intersection of emotional intelligence and AI adoption since 2017 — years before it became a mainstream conversation. The thesis hasn't changed: we keep pouring the foundation last.

MBA and Master of Music — systems thinking meets creative problem-solving. I think in systems, communicate in stories, and ship on deadlines.
