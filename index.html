About

I've spent 20+ years in enterprise technology, and the lesson that keeps proving itself is this: the organizations that win aren't the ones with the best tools. They're the ones that build the operational and cultural infrastructure to actually use them.

At AWS, I built the Southeast professional services practice from 5 to 60+ people. I launched the Healthcare vertical and turned a decade of losses into a profitable practice with 100% customer retention. At Microsoft, I led cloud transformation for 60+ enterprise accounts and turned 6 critical escalations into platform expansions. The pattern was always the same: the technology was never the hard part.

I hold an MBA and a Master of Music. That combination sounds unusual until you realize both are about systems thinking, listening and performing under pressure. I founded Allies@ at Amazon because culture isn't a side project. It's load-bearing infrastructure.

I've been writing about the intersection of emotional intelligence and AI since 2017, years before it became a mainstream conversation. The thesis hasn't changed: AI transformation isn't a technology problem. It's a human and operational one. We keep pouring the foundation last.
