## Page Structure

### 1. Title & Positioning

**Title:** The Intelligence Abundance System

**Subtitle/tagline:** Three frameworks for organizations making the transition from intelligence scarcity to intelligence abundance.

**Opening paragraph (2-3 sentences):**
The core argument: intelligence abundance is the most significant organizational transformation since the industrial revolution, and most organizations are approaching it wrong. They're treating it as a technology problem when it's simultaneously an organizational design problem, a change management problem, and a human development problem. These three frameworks address all three, as a system.

### 2. The Core Thesis (short section)

A tight version of the foundational argument that runs through all three:

- Intelligence was scarce. Now it's abundant. Every organizational structure built for scarcity is a liability.
- Most AI transformations fail because they address only one dimension (usually technology, sometimes process, rarely people).
- Sustainable transformation requires three things simultaneously: a new organizational model, a pathway to get there, and investment in the humans who make it work.
- These three frameworks cover those three dimensions. They were designed to work together.

### 4. How They Connect (the architecture section)

This is the section that doesn't exist anywhere yet. It should map the explicit connections:

**Design → Implementation flow:**
- The Intelligence Abundance Framework defines the target state (diamond, squads, collaboration models)
- The Change Management Framework implements that target state (four phases that build the structure)
- The Human Capability Model develops the people who operate within that structure (seven capabilities)

**Shared concepts that thread through all three:**
- **Psychological safety** — appears as infrastructure (IAF), prerequisite (CMF), and capability enabler (HCM)
- **The squad** — appears as organizational unit (IAF), implementation mechanism (CMF), and learning accelerator (HCM)
- **Constitutional governance** — appears as coordination mechanism (IAF), cultural artifact (CMF), and ethical framework (HCM)
- **The emotional resistance arc** — appears implicitly (IAF), as organizational phenomenon (CMF), and as individual journey (HCM)
- **Collaboration models** — defined (IAF), selected and practiced (CMF), enabled by collaborative intelligence capability (HCM)

**The dependency chain:**
You can read them in any order, but they implement in sequence. The organizational design (IAF) creates the context. The change pathway (CMF) builds the structure. The human development (HCM) sustains it. Skip any layer and the transformation stalls:
- Without organizational design: change effort with no destination
- Without change management: beautiful blueprint with no construction plan
- Without human development: new structure staffed by people optimized for the old one

### 5. Where to Start (reader guidance)

Different entry points for different readers:

- **"I'm a CEO/CHRO trying to understand what AI transformation actually requires"** → Start with the Intelligence Abundance Framework (the what), then Change Management (the how)
- **"I'm leading an AI transformation that's stalling"** → Start with the Change Management Framework, particularly the failure patterns and resistance arc
- **"I'm worried about my people and whether they can make this transition"** → Start with the Human Capability Model
- **"I want the full picture"** → Read in order: Intelligence Abundance → Change Management → Human Capability
